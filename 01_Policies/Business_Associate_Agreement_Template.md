## RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Parties wish to comply with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA"), the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act ("HITECH Act"), and regulations promulgated thereunder (collectively, "HIPAA Rules");

**WHEREAS**, Business Associate may receive, create, maintain, or transmit Protected Health Information ("PHI") on behalf of Covered Entity;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. Definitions

Terms used in this BAA shall have the meanings set forth in the HIPAA Rules. Additionally:

**"Protected Health Information" or "PHI"** means individually identifiable health information as defined in 45 CFR ยง 160.103.

**"Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI"** means PHI that is transmitted or maintained in electronic media.

**"Breach"** means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in violation of the Privacy Rule that compromises the security or privacy of PHI.

**"Security Incident"** means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations.

## 2. Permitted Uses and Disclosures

### 2.1 General

Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only to:
- Perform services under the underlying Agreement
- Comply with this BAA
- As required by law

### 2.2 Specific Uses

Business Associate may:
- Use PHI for proper management and administration
- Disclose PHI if required by law
- Provide data aggregation services (if applicable)

### 2.3 Prohibited Uses

Business Associate shall not:
- Use or disclose PHI in ways not permitted by this BAA
- Use PHI for marketing purposes without authorization
- Sell PHI

## 3. Obligations of Business Associate

### 3.1 Safeguards

Business Associate shall:
- Implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards as required by HIPAA Security Rule
- Ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI
- Protect against reasonably anticipated threats
- Protect against impermissible uses or disclosures
- Ensure workforce compliance

**Per EcoMetricx Security Standards:**
- Encryption at rest (AES-256)
- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+)
- Multi-factor authentication
- Access controls and logging
- Regular security assessments

### 3.2 Reporting

Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity:
- Any use or disclosure not permitted by this BAA
- Any Security Incident
- Any Breach of Unsecured PHI

**Reporting Timeline:**
- Security Incidents: Within **24 hours** of discovery
- Breaches: Within **24 hours** of discovery (without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 days)

### 3.3 Subcontractors

Business Associate shall:
- Ensure subcontractors agree to same restrictions
- Obtain satisfactory assurances from subcontractors
- Maintain written agreements with subcontractors

### 3.4 Access to PHI

Business Associate shall:
- Make PHI available to individuals upon request
- Support Covered Entity's obligations under 45 CFR ยง 164.524
- Respond to requests within 30 days

### 3.5 Amendment

Business Associate shall:
- Make PHI available for amendment
- Support Covered Entity's obligations under 45 CFR ยง 164.526
- Incorporate amendments as directed

### 3.6 Accounting of Disclosures

Business Associate shall:
- Document disclosures as required
- Make information available for accounting
- Support Covered Entity's obligations under 45 CFR ยง 164.528

### 3.7 Access to Records

Business Associate shall make internal practices and records available to Secretary of HHS for compliance determination.

## 4. Obligations of Covered Entity

Covered Entity shall:
- Provide notice of privacy practices limitations
- Notify Business Associate of permission changes
- Notify of restrictions on use or disclosure

## 5. Term and Termination

### 5.1 Term

This BAA is effective upon the Effective Date and continues until terminated.

### 5.2 Termination for Breach

Either Party may terminate upon material breach if:
- Written notice provided
- Breach not cured within 30 days
- Cure is not feasible

### 5.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination, Business Associate shall:
- Return or destroy all PHI (if feasible)
- Extend protections if return/destruction not feasible
- Limit further uses and disclosures

## 6. Miscellaneous

### 6.1 Regulatory References

References to HIPAA Rules include amendments.

### 6.2 Amendment

Modifications must be in writing signed by both Parties.

### 6.3 Survival

Obligations regarding PHI survive termination.

### 6.4 Interpretation

This BAA shall be interpreted consistent with HIPAA Rules.
